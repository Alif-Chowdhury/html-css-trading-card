Mandarin Fish (or Mandarin Dragonet)

Mandarin Fish has no true scales like other fish. It's body is covered in thick, toxic mucus layer. This fish looks colorful and bright.

  • Scientific Name: Synchiropus splendidus
  • Video: Click here
  • Average Lifespan: 10-15 years (wild), 2-4 years (aquarium)
  • Habitat: Indo-Pacific Ocean

The Mandarin dragonet is a small tropical fish famous for its vivid blue, orange, and green patterns, making it one of the most colorful fish in the ocean. Unlike most fish, it does not have true scales but instead has a smooth body covered in a protective mucus layer.

It moves slowly along the ocean floor, almost like it is “walking” across coral reefs while searching for tiny organisms to eat. This fish is generally peaceful and shy, often staying close to rocks and coral for protection.
